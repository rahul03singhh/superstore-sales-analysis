Superstore Sales Data Analysis

This project explores the Superstore retail dataset to understand the factors that influence sales and profitability. The analysis focuses on identifying trends in different regions, product categories, customer segments, and the impact of discounts on profit.

Objectives

The main questions explored in this project include:

Which regions generate the highest sales?

Which product categories produce the most profit?

Which customer segments contribute the most revenue?

How do discounts impact profitability?

How do sales change over time?

Tools Used

Python

Pandas

Matplotlib

Seaborn

Jupyter Notebook

Key Insights

The West region generates the highest total sales among all regions.

Technology products produce the highest profit, making them the most profitable category.

The Consumer segment contributes the largest share of total sales.

Higher discounts often lead to lower or negative profit, indicating potential issues with aggressive discount strategies.

Sales tend to increase toward the later months of the year, suggesting possible seasonal demand patterns.

Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

Reduce excessive discounts on products that already have low profit margins.

Focus marketing efforts on high-performing regions.

Invest more in profitable product categories such as Technology.

Monitor high-discount orders to avoid negative profit.

Dataset

The dataset used in this project is the Sample Superstore dataset, commonly used for learning data analysis and business intelligence.
